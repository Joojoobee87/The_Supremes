Where Did Our Love Go

"Where Did Our Love Go" was the first of 5 consecutive number one hits for The Supremes. Released 1964, the hit reached No.1 position where it stayed for 2 weeks.

Baby Love

"Baby Love" was a number one hit in 1964 released for their second studio album titled "Where Did Our Love Go"

Come See About Me

The hit "Come See About Me", released in 1964, was the third of 5 consecutive No.1s for The Supremes. It topped the charts twice, being replaced in between by the Beatles hit "I Feel Fine".

Stop! In The Name Of Love

The huge hit "Stop! In the Name of Love" was recorded and released in 1965 and features on the album "More Hits by The Supremes", The Supremes 6th album. It was also nominated for a Grammy Award in 1966 for Best Contemporary Rock & Roll Group Vocal Performance

Back In My Arms Again

"Back in My Arms Again" recorded in 1965, was the last of the 5 consecutive hits to reach the No.1 spot on the charts.

I Hear A Symphony

Following their run of consecutive No.1 hits, the following release "Nothing But Heartaches" didn't make it into the top ten. The producers focussed their efforts on The Supremes and their next release "I Hear A Symphony" became their 6th No.1 hit in 1965.

You Can't Hurry Love

Recorded in 1966, "You Can't Hurry Love" topped the US charts as well as making it into the top 5 in the UK. It was also later re-recorded by Phil Collins where it became No.1 in the UK.

You Keep Me Hangin' On

The song "You Keep Me Hangin' On" was not only a big hit for The Supremes in 1966 but has been re-released by several artists since including Vanilla Fudge and later Kim Wilde where it made No.1 for a second time!

Love Is Here And Now You're Gone

The Supremes run of success continued in 1967 with their hit "Love Is Here And Now You're Gone" which was the second consecutive No.1 hit on the album The Supremes Sing Holland Dozier Holland.

The Happening

"The Happening" became The Supremes' 10th No.1 hit in 1967 - it was also the theme song of the film "The Happening" released by Columbia Pictures in the same year which unfortunately did not achieve the same level of success.
